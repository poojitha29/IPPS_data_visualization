Trend of Average covered charges and Total Discharges

Description-

This web page maps two attributes in one chart

  • Total Discharges of each state – Total Number of patients discharged from all the hospitals for all the procedures using a Drug
  • Total Covered Charges of each state – Total Amount of money charged by all the hospitals in a State for all the procedures using a Drug

Instructions on how to interpret the Map
  1. Select a Drug by using the Drop Down list on top right corner named “DRG Definition”. For example- Select first Drug DRG-039.
  2. Select a state from the list under ‘DRG Definition’, for example, select ‘CA’ from States which is denoted by green color.
  3. Hover on the green bar(denoted by green color) in Total Discharges Graph, you will see that the value is 1,715 for California (for Drug DRG-039)
  4. Now look at Average Covered Charges for CA, it is 3,758,360 which means that for 1,715 patients the average sum of money charged by hospitals in California is 3,758,360.
  5. Double click on California to deselect it, as you can see all the bars will get highlighted.
  6. In Total Discharges do you see any other state that discharges more patient than California?
  7. If you look at the peaks in Total Discharges, then Texas denoted by ‘TX’ discharges maximum number of patients (2,871).
  8. Now look at Average Covered Charges for Texas (right under it denoted by light orange color), it is 511,850.
  9. Do you notice the difference in average covered charges of TX and California for Drug DRG-039?
    Even though the Texas discharges number of patients than California, the amount of money charged by the hospital is way less than that charges in California.
    Similarly, you can try comparing other states for different drugs to see which is less expensive.

One Interesting Fact: California is more expensive irrespective of the Drug chosen.
